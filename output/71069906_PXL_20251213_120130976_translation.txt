=== TRANSLATION (English) ===
-28-
The temperature rises above the ignition point of the fuel. 

(C) Combustion Stroke (Refer to Figure 2-3)
With the intake valve (S) and exhaust valve (E) remaining closed, the piston (P) reaches the end of the compression stroke, that is, just before the crank reaches the top dead center, and at the beginning of this stroke, fuel pressurized by the fuel injector is sprayed in a mist form. It comes into contact with the hot air and ignites spontaneously. The fuel undergoes explosive combustion within the cylinder, and the rapid expansion force of the combustion gases pushes the piston down, converting it into rotational force of the crankshaft via the connecting rod. This stroke is called the combustion stroke or power stroke.

(D) Exhaust Stroke (Refer to Figure 2-3)
If the exhaust valve (E) is opened, as the piston rises past the bottom dead center, it expels the combustion gases into the atmosphere, completing the expulsion when it reaches the top dead center. In the combustion stroke, just before the piston (P) reaches the bottom dead center of the crank, the cam actuates the exhaust valve.

During the four strokes (two reciprocations) of the piston (P), the crankshaft rotates twice, and there is only one power stroke during this period.

The 2-4 diagram shows the pressure-volume (PV) diagram of a four-cycle engine. When gas is introduced into the cylinder and the piston is sequentially compressed from position A to position B, or conversely, when the piston is pushed from position B to A by the expansion force of the gas, the diagram generally represents the state change of the gas within the cylinder. Pressure is represented by P on the vertical axis, and volume is represented by V on the horizontal axis, illustrating the relationship between the two (the change in gas volume and pressure within the cylinder).

The volume when compressed is represented as V2, and the pressure is represented as P2. This diagram is called a PV diagram or pressure-volume diagram. When the piston is at position A, the volume is V1, and the pressure is Pi, and when at position B, it is represented on the PV diagram.
